Governing Thinning Damage Factor

Base Metal Thickness

A furnished thickness may be replaced with a measured thickness as a result of a high quality inspection (for thinning and external corrosion, as applicable) and high confidence in the measurement accuracy. Key reasons for replacing the furnished thickness with a measured thickness are:

  • The component service start date when combined with a reasonably conservative corrosion rate predicts an unrealistically high wall loss when the measured wall loss based on quality inspection is much lower than predicted
  • The process conditions differ significantly from historical service conditions that are the basis for historical measured corrosion rate
  • The furnished thickness based on design is significantly different than the thickness measured by a baseline inspection or lack of reliable baseline data

Shape Factor

$\alpha$ is the shape factor for the component type

$\left\{\begin{matrix} \alpha=2 \hspace{3pt}for \hspace{3pt}a \hspace{3pt}cylinder\\ \alpha=4 \hspace{3pt}for\hspace{3pt} a \hspace{3pt}sphere \\ \alpha=1.13\hspace{3pt} for \hspace{3pt}a \hspace{3pt} head \end{matrix}\right.$

Strength Ratio- Internal Pressure/Hoop Stress

$SR_p^{Thin} = \frac{S \times E}{F{S^{Thin}}} \times \frac{Max({t_{\min }},{t_c})}{t_{rdi}}$

$SR_p^{Thin}$ is the strength ratio parameter defined as the ratio of hoop stress to flow stress

S is the allowable stress

E is the allowable stress

$F{S^{Thin}}$ is the Flow Stress

${t_c}$ is the minimum structural thickness of the component base material

$t_{rdi}$ the furnished thickness,t, or measured thickness reading from previous inspection, only if there is a high level of confidence in its accuracy, with respect to wall loss associated with internal corrosion

Flow Stress

$F{S^{Thin}} = \frac{YS + TS}{2} \times E \times 1.1$

YS is the Tensile Strength at design temperature

TS is the Yield Strength at design temperature

E is the weld joint efficiency or quality code from the original construction code

Srength Ratio- Minimum Structural Thickness Governs

$SR_p^{Thin} = \frac{P \times D}{\alpha \times F{S^{Thin} \times {t_{rdi}}}}$

$SR_p^{Thin}$ is the strength ratio parameter defined as the ratio of hoop stress to flow stress

P is the Pressure (operating, design, PRD overpressure, etc.)

D is the component inside diameter

$F{S^{Thin}}$ is the Flow Stress

$t_{rdi}$ the furnished thickness,t, or measured thickness reading from previous inspection, only if there is a high level of confidence in its accuracy, with respect to wall loss associated with internal corrosion

Damage Factor Adjustment for Online Monitoring

Damage Factor Adjustment for Injection Points

If a piping circuit contains an injection/mix point, then an adjustment factor equal to $F_{ip}=3$ should be used to account for the higher likelihood of thinning activity at this location. If a highly effective inspection specifically for injection/mix point corrosion within the injection point circuit (according to API 570) is performed, then an adjustment is not necessary, or $F_{ip}=1$ .

Damage Factor Adjustment for Dead Legs

$F_{DL}$ – A deadleg is defined as a section of piping or piping circuit that is used only during intermittent service such as start-ups, shutdowns, or regeneration cycles rather than continuous service. Deadlegs include components of piping that normally have no significant flow. If a piping circuit contains a deadleg, then an adjustment should be made to the thinning DF to account for the higher likelihood of thinning activity at this location. The adjustment factor is $F_{DL}=3$. If a highly effective inspection method is used to address the potential of localized corrosion in the deadleg, then an adjustment is not necessary, or $F_{DL}=1$
